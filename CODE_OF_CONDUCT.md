# Code of Conduct

## The rule

Behave like an adult who wants the project to succeed.

## What that looks like

- Assume good faith. Most disagreements are misunderstandings, not bad actors.
- Disagree on technical substance, not identity. Attack ideas; don't attack people.
- When wrong, say so. When shown wrong, thank the person who showed you.
- If a newcomer asks a question you find obvious, remember that everyone was once a newcomer.
- English is the working language, but non-native speakers are welcome. Write clearly; read charitably.

## What gets you removed

- Harassment, targeted abuse, doxxing, sustained hostility.
- Unsolicited sexual content or imagery.
- Threats, incitement to violence, or promotion of hate.
- Repeatedly derailing discussions with off-topic or bad-faith arguments.
- Using XRAI venues (issues, discussions, chat) to promote unrelated commercial products.

## Process

1. Report to `conduct@xrai.dev` with a link + short description. Private, not public.
2. Maintainers acknowledge within 72 hours.
3. First serious violation: warning, usually private.
4. Repeated or severe violations: ban from the repo and public channels.

Maintainer decisions are final. We err on the side of protecting contributors who are trying to build something over protecting contributors who are trying to make other people's lives worse.

## Scope

Applies to all XRAI venues: GitHub repo (issues, PRs, discussions, wiki), `xrai.dev`, any official chat or mailing list, and official conference appearances. Applies to both online and offline behavior when representing the project.

## Not this document's job

- Bikeshedding on technical choices (use RFCs).
- General unfriendliness below the "removal" bar (downvote, move on).
- Gatekeeping based on seniority or credentials.
